Six months of building Tanker on top of Claude Code. Open-sourcing it today.

Tanker turns a one-line product brief into a deployed product:
  /cto "AI business analyst for Indian D2C sellers"
  → live URL in ~3 hours
  → ~30 min of my attention total

Why this matters for product leaders:

The cost of completeness with AI is near zero. The bottleneck isn't generation. It's the workflow around generation: defining the right product, writing the PRD, checking quality, deploying.

Tanker wires that workflow into one orchestrator (/cto) with two human gates:
  Gate 1: PRD review (after a review agent pre-qualifies)
  Gate 2: MVP review (after a review agent pre-qualifies)

You see only what's worth seeing.

What's inside (.claude folder you drop into any project):
  • 34 slash skills covering product, design, planning, build, quality
  • 9 specialist agents (review, research, provisioner)
  • 3 always-on rules — including a "No AI Slop" ban list
  • Real provisioning: GitHub, Supabase, Vercel, Railway via APIs
  • Cost ceiling (--max-cost-usd, default $10)
  • Resumable across sessions
  • Local semantic retrieval over your knowledge

Honest comparisons in the docs vs MetaGPT, AutoGen, CrewAI, Aider, gstack.

Repo: https://github.com/tanishg98/tanker
Docs: https://tanker.dev

If you're a product leader using AI tools daily and want to compare notes — DM. The most useful feedback I'm hoping for: would you trust two-gates-with-pre-qualification, or would you want more / fewer gates?

Built solo, on Claude Code, on weekends.
